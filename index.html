Nigerian Jollof Rice

Nigerian Jollof Rice is a popular West African dish made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, onions, and spices. It is commonly served at parties, family gatherings, and celebrations.

Recipe Details

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 45 minutes

Servings: 6 people

Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long grain rice
  • 5 fresh tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 1 onion
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • Vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and set it aside.
  2. Blend the tomatoes, peppers, and onion until smooth.
  3. Heat vegetable oil and fry the tomato paste for a few minutes.
  4. Add the blended mixture, seasoning, curry, thyme, and salt. Cook until the sauce thickens.
  5. Add the rice and enough water or stock, then stir well.
  6. Cover the pot and cook on low heat until the rice becomes soft.
  7. Serve hot with fried chicken, salad, or plantain.
Tips

For the best smoky party-style flavor, cook the rice on low heat after the water has been absorbed.

Avoid stirring the rice too often while it cooks.
